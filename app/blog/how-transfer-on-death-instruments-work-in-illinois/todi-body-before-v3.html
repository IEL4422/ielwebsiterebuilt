How Transfer on Death Instruments Work in Illinois

Table of contents
  1. What Is a Transfer on Death Instrument (TODI)?
  2. How Does a TODI Work?
  3. Benefits of Using a TODI in Illinois
    1. ✅ Avoids Probate
    2. ✅ Retains Full Control During Life
    3. ✅ Simplifies Estate Administration
    4. ✅ Flexible and Revocable
    5. ✅ No Immediate Tax Implications
  4. Limitations and Considerations
    1. ❌ Applies Only to Residential Real Estate
    2. ❌ Subject to Debts and Liens
    3. ❌ Not Ideal for Complex Family Situations
    4. ❌ Must Be Properly Executed and Recorded
  5. How to Create a TODI in Illinois

When it comes to estate planning, many people want to avoid probate and ensure that their assets pass seamlessly to their beneficiaries. One tool available in Illinois for real estate owners is the Transfer on Death Instrument (TODI). This document allows a property owner to name a beneficiary who will automatically inherit the property upon their passing—without the need for probate.
